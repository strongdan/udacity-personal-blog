North Island Brown Kiwi

Author: Daniel Strong

Share:

The kiwi is a small flightless bird, distantly related to ostriches and emus. They have hair-like feathers, large eggs, and nostrils at the tip of their beaks.

  • Scientific Name: Apteryx mantelli
  • Average Height: 40 to 50 cm (16 to 20 inches)
  • Average Lifespan: 25 to 50 years
  • Habitat: Forests, scrublands, and grasslands in New Zealand
Read more on Wikipedia
Interesting Facts
  • Kiwis are flightless birds native to New Zealand, and they use their strong legs to run and dig.
  • They have a highly developed sense of smell, with nostrils located at the tip of their long beaks.
  • Kiwis lay one of the largest eggs in relation to their body size of any bird species.
  • They are nocturnal, meaning they are primarily active at night.
  • Kiwis are a national symbol of New Zealand, and their name is used to refer to the people of New Zealand.
