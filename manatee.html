West Indian Manatee

Author: Daniel Strong

Share:

Manatees are large, aquatic herbivorous mammals that live in shallow waters of the Caribbean, Gulf of Mexico, Amazon basin, and West Africa. They are slow but agile swimmers and can continuously generate their teeth.

  • Scientific Name: Trichechus manatus
  • Average Length: 2.8 to 3.5 meters (9 to 11 feet)
  • Average Lifespan: 40 years
  • Habitat: Coastal waters, rivers, and estuaries
Read more on Wikipedia
Interesting Facts
  • Manatees are herbivores, eating up to 100 pounds of seagrass and plants each day.
  • They are known as "sea cows" because they are slow-moving and gentle, spending most of their time grazing.
  • Manatees need warm water to survive and can get cold stress when temperatures drop below 20°C (68°F).
  • Despite their large size, manatees can swim up to 20 miles per hour in short bursts.
  • Manatees communicate by squealing under water to keep in contact with each other.
