Atlantic Puffin

Puffins can dive to 60 meters (nearly 200 feet)

  • Scientific Name: Fratercula arctica
  • Average Length: 28 to 30 cm (11 to 12 inches)
  • Average Lifespan: 25 years
  • Habitat: Sea cliffs and rocky islands

Puffins are small seabirds that feed by diving in the water and live in large colonies on rocky cliffs. They have short wings adapted for swimming underwater and can beat their wings up to 400 times per minute for low flight.

European Hedgehog

Hedgehogs are carnivorous but lactose intolerant

  • Scientific Name: European Hedgehog
  • Average Length: 10 inches
  • Average Lifespan: 5 years
  • Habitat: woodland edges, hedgerows and suburban habitats

Hedgehogs are small mammals that are born with soft spines which later harden to protect them. They are mostly nocturnal and eat insects, as well as small mice, snails, lizards, frogs, eggs, and even snakes

North Island Brown Kiwi

Kiwis hatch fully feathered and ready to forage on their own

  • Scientific Name: Apteryx mantelli
  • Average Height: 2 feet tall
  • Average Lifespan: 25 - 50 years
  • Habitat: Coniferous forests

The kiwi is a small flightless bird, distantly related to ostriches and emus. They have hair-like feathers, large eggs, and nostrils at the tip of their beaks.

West Indian Manatee

The Manateeʼs closest living relative is the elephant

  • Scientific Name: Trichechus manatus
  • Average Length: 4 meters (13 feet)
  • Average Lifespan: 65 years
  • Habitat: Rivers, bays, and estuaries

Manatees are large, aquatic herbivorous mammals that live in shallow waters of the Caribbean, Gulf of Mexico, Amazon basin, and West Africa. They are slow buy agile swimmers and can continuously generate their teeth.

Nine-banded Armadillo

Armadillos are nocturnal and can spend up to 16 hours sleeping in burrows, sometimes shared with rats, tortoises, and snakes

  • Scientific Name: Dasypus novemcinctus
  • Average Length: 75 cm (30 in)
  • Average Lifespan: 12-15 years
  • Habitat: rain forests, grasslands, and dry scrub

Armadillos are mammals with leathery armor and strong claws for digging. They are distantly related to anteaters and sloths. They give birth once a year to either four males or four females.

Platypus

Platypus glow under black light (biofluoresence) and they donʼt have a true stomach.

  • Scientific Name: Ornithorhynchus anatinus
  • Average Length: 20 inches
  • Average Lifespan: 17 years
  • Habitat: Rivers, streams and other freshwater

The platypus is a egg-laying mammal native to eastern Australia and Tasmania. It uses electrolocation to sense prey in water and males have a venomous spur on their hind legs. They were once hunted for their fur and today their population is threatenened by habitat destruction and pollution
