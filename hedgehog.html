European Hedgehog

Author: Daniel Strong

Share:

Hedgehogs are small mammals that are born with soft spines which later harden to protect them. They are mostly nocturnal and eat insects, as well as small mice, snails, lizards, frogs, eggs, and even snakes

  • Scientific Name: Erinaceus europaeus
  • Average Length: 14 to 30 cm (5.5 to 12 inches)
  • Average Lifespan: 3 to 7 years
  • Habitat: Forests, grasslands, and gardens
Read more on Wikipedia
Interesting Facts
  • Hedgehogs are covered in spines, which they use to protect themselves from predators by curling up into a tight ball.
  • They have poor eyesight but an excellent sense of smell and hearing, which they use to find food.
  • Hedgehogs are nocturnal creatures, meaning they are active during the night and sleep during the day.
  • They are insectivores, primarily feeding on insects, but they also eat small vertebrates and fruits.
  • Hedgehogs can hibernate during colder months if the weather becomes too cold or food becomes scarce.
