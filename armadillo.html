Nine-banded Armadillo

Author: Daniel Strong

Share:

Armadillos are mammals with leathery armor and strong claws for digging. Distantly related to anteaters and sloths, they give birth once a year to either four males or four females.

  • Scientific Name: Dasypus novemcinctus
  • Average Length: 75 cm (30 inches)
  • Average Lifespan: 12-15 years
  • Habitat: Rain forests, grasslands, and dry scrub
Read more on Wikipedia
Interesting Facts
  • Armadillos use their long, sticky tongues to feel around in ant nests, slurping up lots of ants at a time.
  • Nine-banded armadillos can hold their breath for up to six minutes when submerged.
  • Armadillos have poor eyesight but a strong sense of smell, which they use to hunt for food.
  • These animals are good swimmers and can walk underwater across the bottom of rivers.
  • Armadillos can jump 3 to 4 feet in the air if frightened.
