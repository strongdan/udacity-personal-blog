Platypus

Author: Daniel Strong

Share:

The platypus is a unique mammal with a duck-like bill, webbed feet, and beaver-like tail. It is one of the few egg-laying mammals (monotremes) and has the unusual ability to sense electrical signals from its prey while hunting underwater.

  • Scientific Name: Ornithorhynchus anatinus
  • Average Length: 38 to 60 cm (15 to 24 inches)
  • Average Lifespan: 17 years
  • Habitat: Rivers, lakes, and coastal streams
Read more on Wikipedia
Interesting Facts
  • The platypus is one of the few mammals that lays eggs instead of giving birth to live young.
  • Platypuses are venomous; males have a spur on their hind legs that delivers a painful venom.
  • They have a duck-like bill that is actually an incredible sensory organ used to detect prey in muddy water.
  • The platypus has waterproof fur that keeps it warm and dry when swimming in cold waters.
  • Platypuses can close their eyes, ears, and nose when swimming to focus on detecting prey with their bill.
