Atlantic Puffin

Author - Daniel Strong

Share:

Atlantic puffins are small seabirds known for their colorful beaks and distinctive black-and-white plumage. They are excellent swimmers and are commonly found in the North Atlantic

  • Scientific Name: Fratercula arctica
  • Average Length: 75 cm (30 in)
  • Average Lifespan: 12-15 years
  • Habitat: Coastal cliffs, islands, and sea
Read more on Wikipedia

Some interesting facts

  • Nicknamed “sea parrots,” puffins have black and white feathers and a colorful beak that changes from grey in winter to bright orange in spring.
  • Puffins are excellent swimmers and divers, using their wings to “fly” underwater and reaching depths of up to 60 meters.
  • They can flap their wings up to 400 times a minute, flying at speeds up to 88 km/h.
  • Puffins mate for life, returning to the same burrow each year to raise their single chick, known as a “puffling.”
