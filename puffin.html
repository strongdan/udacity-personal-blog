Atlantic Puffin

Author - Daniel Strong

Share:

Atlantic puffins are small seabirds known for their colorful beaks and distinctive black-and-white plumage. They are excellent swimmers and divers, primarily feeding on fish, and are commonly found in the North Atlantic, particularly around Iceland, Norway, and the British Isles

  • Scientific Name: Fratercula arctica
  • Average Length: 75 cm (30 in)
  • Average Lifespan: 12-15 years
  • Habitat: Coastal cliffs, islands, and sea

Puffins are birds known for their colorful beaks and distinctive appearance. They are excellent swimmers and spend most of their lives at sea.
